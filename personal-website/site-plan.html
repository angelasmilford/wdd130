Content

Home page

Talofa lava and Bulavinaka! In English, Greetings and Welcome! M y name is Angela S. Milford, but I am commonly and friendly known as "Teph." I am 19 years old, and I was born and raised in the smallest U.S. Territory in the Pacific: American Samoa. My ancestry ties are in two Pacific Island nations: Samoa and Fiji. On this website, you will get a feel for what life is like in both Samoa and Fiji. I am proud to share with you about my two countries!

Samoa

Talofa lava and Welcome to beautiful Samoa, Visitor! Welcome to the Polynesian side of Angela S. Milford. Here, you will be learning about what Samoa is all about! For clarity, it is important to note that there are two Samoas, one of which is a sovereign nation and the other is an unincorporated territory of the United States. Samoa is an independent nation in the South Pacific Ocean, consisting of four main islands: Savai'i, Upolu, Apolima, and Manono. It is a Polynesian nation located in the central South Pacific Ocean, earning it the nickname "the heart of the Pacific." Samoa, like many other Pacific Islands, is rich in culture, history, and natural beauty. Samoa is rich in cultural heritage. The Samoan chiefly system, called the “Matai” is a hierarchical system of governance in which the chief leads the village. Samoa is the only Samoan island that recognizes both genders in the Matai system. Samoan traditions, core values, dances, tattoos, and language are still intact and vibrant. To learn about Samoa’s rich culture, press this button. The national flag and government sigil of Samoa are depicted here. Samoa gained independence in 1962, after a period as a German and then a New Zealand territory. Samoa is a democratic nation with a unicameral legislature. Samoa recently made history by announcing its first female Prime Minister, Fiame Naomi Mataafa. To learn more about Samoa’s history, press this button. Life in Samoa is arduous. Samoa is more hectic than American Samoa, with everyone on their feet, hustling for money to support themselves or their families. Crops grown at their homes, such as taro, breadfruit, mango, papaya, chocolate, and seafood, are sold for monetary gain. The crops are harvested after a great deal of physical labor, which explains why Samoans appear so physically fit to outsiders. To learn Samoa’s way of life, watch the video by clicking this button.

Fiji

Bulavinaka and Welcome to Fiji. Welcome to the Melanesian side of Angela S. Milford. Here, you will be learning about what Fiji is all about! Fiji is a Melanesian nation in the South Pacific Ocean, comprising more than 300 islands. The archipelago is widely recognized for its well-deserved reputation as “the soft coral capital of the world.” The capital of Fiji is Suva, which is on its main island. Fiji has four districts, each of which is further subdivided into thirteen provinces. Due to the country’s many provinces, there are a variety of dialects spoken; however, the main dialect practiced is called “Bauan.” Fiji, like many other Pacific nations, is rich in culture, history, and natural resources. The national flag and the government sigil of Fiji are depicted in this image. Fiji gained its independence in 1970, following its cession to the United Kingdom by the first King Cakobau. Fiji’s government is a parliamentary democracy that recently elected a new Prime Minister, Sitiveni Rabuka, after seventeen consecutive years.This image depicts the national flag and government sigil of Fiji. Fiji gained independence in 1970, following its cession to the United Kingdom by the first King Cakobau. Fiji's government is a parliamentary democracy that recently elected a new Prime Minister, Sitiveni Rabuka, after seventeen consecutive years. To learn about Fiji’s history, press this button. Life in Fiji is similar to Samoa. Because of their commercial activities, Fijians are offered many job opportunities.Most residents either work in commercial enterprises or sell their crops at flea markets. Fiji is renowned for its hospitality, and as a result of its many beautiful sites, it draws many tourists who are treated with the utmost care.

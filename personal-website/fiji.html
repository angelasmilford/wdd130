Fiji

Fiji is a Melanesian nation in the South Pacific Ocean, comprising more than 300 islands. The archipelago is widely recognized for its well-deserved reputation as “the soft coral capital of the world.” The capital of Fiji is Suva, which is on its main island. Fiji has four districts, each of which is further subdivided into thirteen provinces. Due to the country’s many provinces, there are a variety of dialects spoken; however, the main dialect practiced is called “Bauan.” Fiji, like many other Pacific nations, is rich in culture, history, and natural resources.

History

Fiji gained its independence in 1970, following its cession to the United Kingdom by the first King Cakobau. Fiji’s government is a parliamentary democracy that recently elected a new Prime Minister, Sitiveni Rabuka, after seventeen consecutive years. Culture

Fiji is rich in cultural heritage. The Fijian chiefdom is a hierarchy system practiced in villages where residents elect their leaders. The system recognizes both genders. While some traditions are lost due to Christianity, Fiji’s traditions, core values, dances, tattoos, and language are still intact. The system recognizes both genders, and while some traditions have been lost due to the influence of Christianity, Fiji's traditions, core values, dances, tattoos, and language remain largely intact.

Way of Life

Life in Fiji is similar to Samoa. Because of their commercial activities, Fijians are offered many job opportunities. Most residents either work in commercial enterprises or sell their crops at flea markets. Fiji is renowned for its hospitality, and as a result of its many beautiful sites, it draws many tourists who are treated with the utmost care.

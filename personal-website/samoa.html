Samoa

For clarity, it is important to note that there are two Samoas, one of which is a sovereign nation and the other is an unincorporated territory of the United States. Samoa is an independent nation in the South Pacific Ocean, consisting of four main islands: Savai'i, Upolu, Apolima, and Manono. It is a Polynesian nation located in the central South Pacific Ocean, earning it the nickname "the heart of the Pacific." Samoa, like many other Pacific Islands, is rich in culture, history, and natural beauty.

Visit Samoa

History

Samoa gained independence in 1962, after a period as a German and then a New Zealand territory. Samoa is a democratic nation with a unicameral legislature. Samoa recently made history by announcing its first female Prime Minister, Fiame Naomi Mataafa.

Culture

Samoa is rich in cultural heritage. The Samoan chiefly system, called the “Matai,” is a hierarchical system of governance in which the chief leads the village. Samoa is the only Samoan island that recognizes both genders in the Matai system. Samoan traditions, core values, dances, tattoos, and language are still intact and vibrant.

Way of Life

Life in Samoa is arduous. Samoa is more hectic than American Samoa, with everyone on their feet, hustling for money to support themselves or their families. Crops grown at their homes, such as taro, breadfruit, mango, papaya, and chocolate, as well as seafood captured from the sea are sold for monetary gain. The crops are harvested after a great deal of physical labor, which explains why Samoans appear so physically fit to outsiders.
